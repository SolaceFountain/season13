Immunity is back up for grabs after a fun $wap yesterday! Today's challenge is called Greed. And nope it's not the greed challenge that you may have seen in other ORGs lately. Ours here literally tests you how greedy you are when it comes to money and tribal immunity. Feeling like you may have been spending too much in the first five rounds? Needing a monetary boost in your balance? This could be the perfect challenge for you, depending on how safe you feel you are in your new tribe... For this challenge, we are offering all of you the temptation to grab up to $5,200 for yourself, instead of spending money for tribal immunity. It’s up to yourself to decide how much money you want to take for your own account at the expense of your tribe. The minimum amount to take from the fresh fund is $1 and the maximum is $5,200 per player.
What’s the catch? Well, the more money you take away from your tribe, the more likely your tribe will be going to tribal council. The tribe with the lower average amount of money taken by its own members will win immunity. If there was a tie for the average amount, standard deviation of the individual amounts taken would be used to break the tie where lower standard deviation would win. If there was a tie in both total and standard deviation (for example when all players on both tribes chose to take the min amount $1), a random rock draw by @$urvivor Goddess would be used. Another catch to note is that if the average amount of money taken away by your tribe exceeds $2,600, all the money taken by everyone in your tribe would be voided. So you may want to think twice before getting too greedy or you and your tribemates would all end up getting no money and still lose immunity. All individual decisions for this challenge will NOT be revealed. We will only reveal the average amount of money taken for each tribe and the standard deviation for each tribe. You are free to discuss privately with your tribemates on how much you’re thinking to take and more than welcome to use #final-15-immunity to negotiate openly with the other tribe. Fun fact from last $eason when we first had this challenge: 2 guys took nearly the maximum they could take; Sean was blindsided after losing this challenge and Dan went on to win the game! Hope it's all clear. Any questions or doubts, please ask. Decision deadline: 9:30 AM (EST) tomorrow.

• You have to decide how much you want to bid to help your tribe to win tribal immunity.
• Minimum is $1.
• Your individual bid will be deducted from your balance and it shall remain confidential.
• Only the average bid for each tribe will be revealed. 
Part 2: Guesstimate
• You have to guesstimate the average amount bid by the opposing tribe for Part 1.
• Your individual guesstimate WILL be revealed. It will be used to calculate the average guesstimate submitted by your tribe.
• This average guesstimate will then be compared with the actual average bid of the opposing tribe to measure how close your guesstimate is to the actual.
• Whichever tribe with a more accurate guess to how much the other tribe bid on average would win a boost of 13% to the amount they bid in Part 1.
Example: The average bid for Tribe P in Part 1 is $1,000. The average bid for Tribe V in Part 1 is $1,100. On average Tribe P guesstimate that Tribe V bid $2,000 for Part 1. On average Tribe V guesstimate that Tribe P bid $3,000 for Part 1. Tribe P made a more acccurate guess than Tribe V (2000-1100 vs 3000-1000) and thus their average bid for Part 1 would get a 13% boost. Tribe P's amount that counts towards winning immunity would be $1,130 while Tribe V's amount remain at $1,100. In this example, Tribe P would win tribal immunity ($1,130 > $1,100) and the votes they had cast in this tribal council WOULD NOT BE READ. Tribe V's votes against each other would be read right after the immunity results had been announced. Remember, during this "live" tribal council, you can only send up to 20 private messages to anyone in the Final 14 via the whisper system as explained in #final-14-whispers-log. Each whisper would cost you $130, to be deducted from your game balance. In summary, all 14 of you have to submit 3 things in your private decisions channel by 11:00AM (EDT), Mar 10th 2020:
1) Bid for tribal immunity
2) Guess the average bid submitted by the opposing tribe
